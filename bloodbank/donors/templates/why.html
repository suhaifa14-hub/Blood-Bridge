Reasons to Donate

Donating blood is one of the most direct ways to save another person's life.

🆘

Emergency Trauma

Accident victims often need large transfusions within minutes. Donated blood on the shelf is the only thing that saves them.

👶

Pregnancy Complications

During emergency C-section or other pregnancy complication ,mothers need emergency blood . There is no substitute — only human donation works.

⚕️

Major Surgeries

Heart surgeries and organ transplants routinely require transfusions. Without supply, these operations cannot happen.

💊

Cancer Patients

Chemotherapy destroys blood cells. Cancer patients often need multiple transfusions throughout their treatment.

🧬

Chronic Conditions like Thalassemia or Anemia

Patients with sickle cell disease or thalassemia, anemia depend on regular transfusions — sometimes every few weeks.

❤️

Your Own Health

Donating stimulates new blood cell production and gives you a free mini health check including blood pressure and hemoglobin.

Who Can Donate?

Most healthy adults can donate. Here's a quick guide.

✅ You Can Donate If...

  • You are 18–65 years old
  • Your weight is at least 50 kg
  • You feel healthy on the day of donation
  • Your hemoglobin meets the minimum level
  • It has been at least 3 months (male) or 4 months (female) since your last donation
  • No tattoo or piercing in the last 6 months

⛔ Do Not Donate If...

  • You had a cold, flu or fever in the last 7 days
  • You are on antibiotics or certain medications
  • You are pregnant or gave birth within 6 months
  • You have a history of HIV or hepatitis B/C
  • You travelled to a malaria zone in the last year
  • You had major surgery in the last 6 months
